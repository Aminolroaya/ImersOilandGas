Introduction

In the oil and gas field, the ability to visualize and see the big picture has always been a valued goal. Immersive technologies can facilitate well planning and visualization, decrease the risk of training in the physical environment by immersive training, increase the details in the on-site training procedure in the oil and gas field, help the training of landing a jackup oil rig, etc.

This research will focus on the applications of immersive technologies (AR/VR/MR) in the oil and gas industry. State of the art papers will be reviewed. Also, the future perspectives will also be illustrated, and a paper will be suggested to read. Some videos related to the applications of immersive technologies in the oil and gas field are provided.

State of the Art

The virtual representation of reservoir models can contribute to more informed decision making such as modeling and prediction of fluid behavior or optimal locations of drilling wells. Sultanum et al. [1] describe the development of a set of novel techniques for supporting 3D representation and exploration of reservoir flow simulation models on tabletops which could provide insight into reservoir models. These techniques are as follows:

  • Tangible cell probe: pinpointing a certain cell and display its specific information

  • Reservoir splitting: Providing a single cutting plane that splits the model

  • Reservoir peeling: Curling the internal faces of sections outwards and in the opposite direction

  • Focus and context for wells: Highlighting an object of interest while also keeping the surrounding context in perspective

Mota et al. [2] proposed a tool that uses analysis of reservoir data to help determine superior well placement and leveraged VR on consumer's head mounted displays and industrial visualization centers. Their tool provides applications such as system control and manipulation (e.g. rotation, scaling), 3-dimensional well creation and trajectory, etc.

PlanWell interface could be applied to many domains like oil and gas. PlanWell prototype can contribute to petroleum engineering tasks such as well planning and drilling operations by providing spatial augmented reality interface that facilitates collaborative field operations. An overseer (in a command and control center) and explorer (an outdoor user in the field) could exchange information based on PlanWell. The overseer uses a scaled 3D printed model of the petroleum reservoir surface terrain to understand the spatial nuances of the terrain, and the explorer interacts with the surface environment directly via an AR interface. It is proved that PlanWell provides value to collaborative petroleum engineering workflows [3].

Virtually training engineers in oil and gas field can prevent the risk of to be injured or killed in a real hazardous environment while training. Building an interactive virtual reality training environment in oil and gas field could be challenging and need several details in order to really have a training procedure. Using a virtual reality training environment proposes more user interactions in comparison with the nowadays training based on photos and videos [4].

Image 1: Simulation Training in Oil Platforms.

In contrast with the above-mentioned procedure for training petroleum engineers, Träskbäck et al. [5] proposed a mixed reality application could be used on-site rather than classrooms. Users can virtually see “inside” the columns and can modify virtually the process. The position accuracy has to be very good for safety reasons in the outdoor training industrial environment, and using mixed reality would be a good solution due to providing situation and position awareness. This application will help users to virtually try out different control values.

Image 2: Mixed reality training application for an oil refinery.

The training purposes in the oil and gas domain is not limited to oil and gas explorations in solid terrains. Also, the process of landing a jackup oil rig in the ocean can be trained via virtual reality software applications [6]. They successfully considered unpredictable weather conditions, varying (deep) ocean forces, and uneven seabed topography for developing the tool. However, how much it is practical in the real world is a big question!

Future Directions

Both industry and engineers will be affected by the leverage of immersive technologies in the oil and gas field. As mentioned, by using these technologies for training students, the risk of participating in dangerous training environment will decrease because engineers can be trained virtually. Also, using immersive technologies on-site will help engineers better understand the details of oil and gas tools such as pipelines or devices they could see through visualized by immersive technologies. Moreover, by using immersive technologies, the places could not be analyzed before can be visualized now. For example, they can see the oil extraction under the oceans, or they can see through the wells virtually. Also, some oil explorations and productions cause injuries, so like remote surgeries that could be done by the help of immersive technologies, it would be great to have a remote virtual oil and gas explorations. In [7], they introduce SimUEP which is a sample of the virtual reality technique in a robotics framework contributing to planning the offshore platform robotizing; However, the idea is on paper, and how much this idea can be applied in the future depends on developing the idea in the real world.

In many papers, the computer scientists tried to introduce immersive applications for the oil and gas domain, while in some papers, petroleum engineers developed the oil and gas related applications in VR/MR/AR. this may contribute to two different ontologies. I believe that by having a unique framework in which both oil and gas experts and computer science developers can communicate and share their pieces of knowledge, the development of immersive technologies in the oil and gas domain can upgrade to the next level.
